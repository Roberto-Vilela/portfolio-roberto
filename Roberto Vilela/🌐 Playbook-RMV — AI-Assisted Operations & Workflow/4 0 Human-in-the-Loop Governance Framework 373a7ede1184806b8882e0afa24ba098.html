RACI, guardrails, human validation, and risk controls.

⚖️ The Core Philosophy: AI as an Assistant, Not an Authority

Drawing from my experience organizing complex client-facing workflows and service delivery operations, I approach technology with a focus on operational safety. I do not believe in unchecked AI autonomy. Unmonitored AI autonomy creates invisible risk; when outputs are accepted at face value, hallucinations become operational facts, and accountability disappears.

My philosophy is simple: Human-in-the-Loop is not just a methodology; it is a professional responsibility. AI accelerates execution, but human validation remains the absolute source of truth for quality and risk control.

🔄 The Execution Loop (The RACI Model)

To ensure this framework is repeatable and auditable, I designed the execution flow using a clear separation of responsibilities, similar to a corporate RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed). In my workflow, the roles are strictly defined:

  • The Planning Assistant (ChatGPT): Responsible for strategy, analyzing terminal logs, structuring the logic, and documenting the execution.
  • The Execution Agent (OpenCode / DeepSeek): Responsible for the technical heavy lifting—writing code, executing scripts, and interacting with the local DuckDB database.
  • The Decision Maker (Me - The Operator): Accountable for defining the problem, setting the constraints, auditing the generated evidence, and providing the ultimate approval.

🛡️ The Governance Guardrails

The Playbook-RMV application enforces quality control through three specific, hardcoded validation gates:

  1. Evidence-Based Auditing: I never accept AI outputs blindly. Before any asset is saved into the Corporate Knowledge Base, I review the "Real-World Evidence" (terminal logs, test results, and hardware telemetry). The evidence must match the AI's claim.
  1. The Correction Loop: If the output is flawed, it does not move forward. The error log is fed back to the Planning Assistant to refine the execution, creating a continuous improvement cycle.
  1. The Final Governance Gate: The workflow is designed so that the final state — the "Validated Asset" — can only be reached through explicit human approval. The AI recommends, but it does not approve.
