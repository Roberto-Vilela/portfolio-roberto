5.0 System Architecture & Data Engineering

Orchestration, structured persistence, and execution logging.

🏗️ Systems Thinking in Practice

As an AI-assisted systems and workflow builder, my role is to connect the dots. I do not build software just for the sake of writing code; I design architectures that solve operational bottlenecks.

In Playbook-RMV, the architecture was engineered to guarantee three things: seamless orchestration, permanent knowledge retention, and absolute traceability. Here is how the system is structured behind the scenes to deliver a governed workflow.

⚙️ 1. Orchestration & User Interface

  • The Component: A streamlined visual interface built with Streamlit, orchestrated by Python, and connected to local LLMs via llama.cpp.
  • The Operational Value: I moved the AI execution out of a chaotic terminal and into a controlled, visual environment. The interface guides the human operator, ensuring that inputs are standardized and executions are frictionless. By abstracting the technical complexity of local models, the operator can focus entirely on strategy, prompt design, and validation.

💾 2. Structured Persistence (The Knowledge Base)

  • The Component: A lightweight, highly efficient analytical database (DuckDB) acting as the storage engine.
  • The Operational Value: Instead of relying on scattered text files or individual memory, every prompt, response, and metadata point is systematically saved into structured tables. This transforms volatile experiments into a true Corporate Knowledge Base. The data is instantly searchable, highly available, and ready to be exported for operational reporting or compliance audits.

📡 3. Execution Logging & Telemetry

  • The Component: Automated hardware diagnostics and context capture modules integrated into the execution script.
  • The Operational Value: Every time an output is generated, the system automatically logs the exact model used, alongside CPU, RAM, and GPU/VRAM consumption. This completely eliminates the "black box" effect. It creates a permanent, auditable trail that leadership can use to understand compute costs, plan infrastructure capacity, and ensure operational predictability.

💡 The Outcome: A Connected Operational System

By integrating a clear interface, a structured database, and automated logging, the architecture proves that local AI can be governed. The technology serves the process, ensuring that no valuable data is ever lost and every execution is fully traceable.
