The scan creates the insight. The system creates the behavior.

DexaFit clients may experience a strong post-scan motivation spike, but that momentum can fade when the data is not translated into a clear behavioral path.

A digital Wellness Hub that connects scan results, education, AI-assisted guidance, human review, habit tracking, retest preparation, and referral incentives into a structured 90-day loop.

Workflow Diagnosis

The retention issue is not the scan experience. The issue is the lack of a structured post-scan workflow that turns insight into guided behavior.

A scan tells the client where they are. A retention workflow tells them what to do next, when to act, and why returning for a retest matters.

The 90-Day Loop Framework

The Wellness Hub would organize the client journey into three behavioral phases: Understand, Engage, and Prove.

Timeline: Days 1—7

Client Goal:Convert scan results into clarity.

System Function: Interactive report, metric explanation, personalized education, and priority insights.

Business Value: Trust, activation, and early engagement.

Timeline: Days 8—30

Client Goal: Build consistency through guided action.

System Function: AI-assisted action plan, habit tracking, wearable inputs, and education prompts.

Business Value: Ongoing engagement, product adoption, and increased lifetime value.

Timeline: Days 31—90

Client Goal: Prepare for measurable comparison.

System Function: Retest reminders, progress summary, comparison dashboard, and referral prompt.

Business Value: Repeat scan bookings, retention, and referral growth.

Phase Logic Behind the Walkthrough

Phase 1 — Understand

Main Theme: Turning scan data into clarity.

Main Challenge: The client may receive powerful body data but still feel unsure about what the numbers mean or what should be prioritized first.

Mitigation: The Wellness Hub replaces a static report with guided interpretation, plain-language explanations, priority markers, and a clear first action path.

Expected Future State: The client leaves the first week with confidence, context, and one clear next step instead of data confusion.

Phase 2 — Engage

Main Theme: Turning initial motivation into weekly consistency.

Main Challenge: Motivation can fade after the scan if the client does not receive structured guidance, reminders, feedback, or relevant next actions.

Mitigation: The Hub uses AI-assisted next-best-action guidance, habit tracking, education prompts, progress scoring, and partner/service recommendations to keep the client connected to their baseline.

Expected Future State: The client moves from passive understanding to active participation, building consistent behavior that supports measurable progress.

Phase 3 — Prove

Main Theme: Turning progress into measurable validation.

Main Challenge: The client may not return for a retest if the second scan feels like a separate sales push instead of a natural part of the transformation journey.

Mitigation: The system introduces retest countdowns, progress summaries, before-and-after previews, achievement markers, referral prompts, and Baseline Points incentives.

Expected Future State: The second scan becomes the natural next step. The client returns because they want proof of progress, not because they were pressured to buy again.

From Governance Logic to Product Experience

The Intelligence Layer defines how the system should think before it speaks to the user.

After raw scan data, wearable signals, and user-logged inputs are interpreted, the product experience must translate that intelligence into a simple interface: clear status, prioritized actions, review checkpoints, and progress visibility.

The goal is not to expose the full complexity of the system to the client. The goal is to make the next step feel obvious, safe, and connected to their baseline.

This is where the Wellness Hub becomes more than a report dashboard. It becomes a guided post-scan command center.

Interface Elements That Connect Strategy to Product Logic

The dashboard is not only a visual presentation layer. It is the place where the business logic of the 90-Day Action Loop becomes usable for the client. Each core interface element was designed to support one strategic objective: reduce confusion, increase guided engagement, and make the retest feel like a natural next step.

Framework connection: Engage

Product role: Converts insight into guided behavior

Business value: Ongoing engagement and product adoption

The Recommended Next Actions module translates scan interpretation into practical guidance. Instead of leaving the client with multiple possible directions, the interface prioritizes clear action paths connected to the user's baseline.

This connects directly to the Engage phase of the 90-Day Action Loop. The user moves from passive understanding into active participation through guided actions, education prompts, retest reminders, and progress validation.

The module also surfaces a Partner Ecosystem layer — connecting clients with trainers, nutritionists, recovery services, and supplement guidance based on their scan baseline. Partner recommendations are filtered by the client's priority metrics and subject to human review before delivery.

Business logic connection: reducing decision friction increases engagement. When the user knows what to do next, the product becomes a behavioral support system rather than a static report.

Framework connection: Understand

Product role: Converts scan data into clarity

Business value: Trust and early activation

The Baseline Snapshot gives the client a structured view of their current state. It organizes body composition, metabolic, and performance indicators into a dashboard-first experience, reducing the confusion that often happens after receiving scan results.

Instead of showing raw numbers without direction, the interface creates the first layer of interpretation: where the user is now, which metrics matter, and why the next action should feel connected to their baseline.

Business logic connection: clarity builds trust. When users understand their baseline, they are more likely to stay engaged after the scan instead of dropping off with data confusion.

Framework connection: Engage

Product role: Tracks user engagement and action completion

Business value: Habit formation, product adoption, and retention

The Action Score turns daily participation into visible feedback. Instead of allowing the Wellness Hub to function as a passive dashboard, this module helps the user see whether they are consistently engaging with the recommended actions, education prompts, habit tracking, and progress path.

This connects directly to the Engage phase of the 90-Day Action Loop. The user checks the dashboard, receives a recommended action, completes the task, and sees their progress reflected through an engagement or action score.

Business logic connection: visible feedback reinforces behavior. When users can see their participation improving over time, the product becomes a loop of action, reward, and continued engagement rather than a one-time scan report.

Framework connection: Prove

Product role: Converts progress into measurable validation

Business value: Repeat scan bookings and retention

The Retest Readiness module helps the client understand when and why the next scan matters. By combining retest status, timing, and progress comparison, the interface reframes the second scan as part of the transformation journey rather than a separate sales event.

The Progress Comparison view strengthens this logic by showing measurable change over time. Instead of relying on motivation alone, the product gives the user visible proof of improvement across key indicators.

Business Value

Business Value: Turning the Dashboard Into a Retention System

The Wellness Hub transforms the post-scan experience from a one-time report into an ongoing engagement loop.

For the client, the value is clarity, guidance, visible progress, and trust. The system helps users understand their baseline, know what to do next, track their participation, and prepare for measurable comparison.

For the business, the value is higher post-scan engagement, stronger product adoption, increased retest readiness, and greater lifetime value potential. The dashboard gives users a reason to return, act, compare, and continue the journey.

Strategic takeaway: the scan creates the insight, but the system creates the behavior. By turning data into clarity, clarity into action, and action into proof, the Wellness Hub becomes a structured retention engine.
