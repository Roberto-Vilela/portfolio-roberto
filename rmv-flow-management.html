bolt 02 / Product Snapshot

A quick overview of the Management system, its operational purpose, core structure, and role within the ecosystem.

Operational Problem

Immigration operations often rely on scattered information — client details in one place, documents in another, tasks tracked manually, and case status unclear without searching across multiple channels.

Product Approach

The Management system centralizes all case activity into one structured workflow. Clients, cases, documents, forms, tasks, and status are organized in a single operational environment before AI assistance becomes relevant.

Core Structure

Web-based system with client profiles, case records, document tracking, form management, task assignments, and status visibility — built with FastAPI backend, PostgreSQL database, and React frontend.

My Role

I designed the system architecture, workflow logic, data model, and operational structure. The system was built to serve as the source of truth for the RMV Flow Ecosystem.

03 / What the Management System Solves

Operational structure comes before AI assistance.

Before AI can help staff work more efficiently, the operational data needs to be organized. The Management system creates a structured environment where client information, case records, documents, forms, tasks, and progress status are connected in one workflow.

grid_view Operational Pain Points

group Scattered Client Data

Client details, contact history, and case-specific context spread across emails, notes, and separate systems make it harder to serve each case efficiently.

description Disconnected Documents

Documents arrive in different formats and channels, making it difficult to track what was requested, submitted, reviewed, or rejected.

dynamic_form Unstructured Forms

Client intake information needs to move from raw answers into structured data that can support case preparation, review, and follow-up.

checklist Invisible Tasks

Without structured task tracking, staff may lose visibility into what needs to happen next, who is responsible, and which items require follow-up.

visibility Unclear Status

Without structured status updates, managers and staff may struggle to understand case progress, identify bottlenecks, or measure operational performance.

verified_user Human Review Gaps

Sensitive steps require human validation. Without structured review points, important checks can be missed or applied inconsistently across cases.

The Management system is the foundation. Without structured operations, AI assistance cannot reference reliable context, and human review cannot be applied consistently.

04 / Why a Dedicated Management Layer

The system is separated from AI to keep operations reliable.

The Management system is not embedded in the AI interface. It operates as an independent layer so that case data, document tracking, and workflow logic remain stable and reliable — regardless of changes to the AI layer or the MCP bridge.

Operational Stability

Design Choice

The Management system is the source of truth. It does not depend on AI availability or MCP connectivity to function. Case operations continue even when AI tools are offline.

Result

Operations never depend on AI uptime

Clean Data Foundation

Design Choice

Data is structured first — clients, cases, documents, forms, tasks, and status are organized in a relational model before any AI layer interacts with them.

Result

AI tools work with structured data, not raw noise

Human Control

Design Choice

All sensitive actions — case updates, communication, status changes — happen inside the Management system. AI tools access data through controlled MCP tools, not direct system manipulation.

Result

Staff maintain full control over operational decisions
